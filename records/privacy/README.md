# Privacy Record Templates

These templates support GDPR and Swiss nFADP controls under `SOP-023` when those controls are in scope for the adopting company's quality manual.

They are intentionally product-independent. An adopting company should instantiate the records needed for its processing scope and should record the scope rationale when personal data processing, jurisdictional scope, or contractual commitments are not yet in scope.

## Templates

| Template | Use |
|---|---|
| `processing_activity_register_template.md` | Records privacy applicability, controller/processor role, processing activities, recipients, retention, security measures, and jurisdictional scope. |
| `lawful_basis_assessment_template.md` | Documents lawful basis, special-category condition, transparency, consent, legitimate-interest, and Swiss justification decisions. |
| `dpia_screening_template.md` | Screens processing activities for high-risk triggers and records the DPIA decision. |
| `dpia_template.md` | Captures the DPIA when high-risk processing is identified. |
| `data_subject_request_log_template.md` | Tracks rights requests, identity verification, response timing, decision rationale, and closure. |
| `personal_data_breach_register_template.md` | Records personal data breach assessment, notification decisions, and follow-up actions. |
| `transfer_assessment_template.md` | Records EEA/Swiss cross-border transfer mechanisms, safeguards, and review decisions. |

## Applicability Notes

- Use `not_in_scope_no_personal_data` when the controlled scope does not process personal data.
- Use `not_yet_in_scope_before_personal_data_processing` when privacy records must be completed before in-scope processing begins.
- Use `in_scope_controller`, `in_scope_processor`, `in_scope_joint_controller`, or `in_scope_mixed_roles_by_processing_activity` when GDPR or Swiss nFADP obligations apply.
- Product, project, or customer-specific privacy records belong in the designated product/project/customer record repository unless the adopting company deliberately keeps them in its company QMS repository.
