## 1. Purpose
Define how ACME identifies, evaluates, and reports adverse events/incidents to regulatory authorities within applicable jurisdictional timelines.

## 2. Scope
Applies to post-release product events that may require mandatory reporting in Switzerland, EU, or US markets.

## 3. Inputs
- Complaint and feedback cases
- PMS signal assessments
- Product risk files and known hazard controls

## 4. Outputs
- Reportability decision record
- Regulatory submission package and submission evidence
- Follow-up/corrective action linkage

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Regulatory Lead | Detects and triages potential incidents. |
| Management Representative | Approves reportability decisions and submissions. |
| QA Lead | Ensures process compliance and record completeness. |
| Engineering/Medical Experts | Provide technical and clinical impact assessments. |

## 6. Procedure

### 6.1 Event Detection and Immediate Escalation
1. Flag any event suggesting serious deterioration, injury risk, or significant malfunction recurrence.
2. Escalate potential reportable events to management representative without delay.

### 6.2 Reportability Assessment
1. Assess event facts against jurisdictional reportability rules maintained in product dossier.
2. Document decision rationale, including uncertainty and assumptions.
3. For ambiguous cases, classify as reportable unless justified otherwise by qualified review.

### 6.3 Submission Preparation
1. Compile required facts: event narrative, device/software identifiers, risk assessment, corrective actions, and known occurrences.
2. Ensure consistency with complaint/CAPA/PMS records.
3. Submit to relevant authority within required timeline.

### 6.4 Follow-Up and Field Actions
1. Track follow-up requests from authorities.
2. Link incident outcomes to CAPA and product change controls.
3. Where needed, coordinate customer communication and field corrective action.

### 6.5 Trend Integration
1. Feed incident data into PMS trend analysis and management review package.
2. Reassess risk controls when incident pattern changes.

## 7. Required Records
- Incident intake and reportability assessment
- Regulatory submission evidence and authority correspondence
- Linked CAPA/change and closure summary
