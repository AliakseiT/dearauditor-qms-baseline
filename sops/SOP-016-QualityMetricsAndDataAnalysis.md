## 1. Purpose
Define quality metric selection, collection, analysis, and escalation to support objective quality management decisions and continuous improvement.

## 2. Scope
Applies to QMS and product quality indicators used in operational monitoring and management review.

## 3. Inputs
- Metric definitions and targets from quality objective framework
- Process/product data sources
- Audit, CAPA, complaint, incident, and PMS outputs

## 4. Outputs
- Periodic quality metrics dashboard/report
- Escalations and action recommendations
- Management review input package

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
| Role | Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| Management Representative | Owns metric governance and management reporting. |
| QA Lead | Validates data quality and action follow-up. |
| Role Owners | Provide source data and investigate out-of-target metrics for assigned processes. |

## 6. Procedure

### 6.1 Metric Definition
1. Define each metric with ID, formula, owner, frequency, and target.
2. Ensure each quality objective has at least one measurable indicator.
3. Document rationale for threshold values.

### 6.2 Data Collection
1. Collect data on defined cadence (weekly/monthly/quarterly).
2. Validate completeness and consistency of source data.
3. Correct or annotate data quality issues before publication.

### 6.3 Analysis
1. Evaluate current value against target and prior trend.
2. Classify status as `on-target`, `warning`, or `out-of-control`.
3. Perform root-cause-oriented analysis for sustained negative trends.

### 6.4 Escalation and Actions
1. Open action/CAPA where thresholds are breached.
2. Track action completion and effectiveness.
3. Present significant deviations in management review.

### 6.5 Periodic Review
1. Review metric set at least annually for relevance and effectiveness.
2. Retire/add metrics based on process maturity and risk profile.

## 7. Required Records
- Metric definitions and targets
- Periodic reports and trend analyses
- Escalation/action linkage evidence
